Add the first slice of the axiom rules-engine backend (`src/axiom/`): policyengine-uk-rust can now evaluate statute-derived RuleSpec rules (composed from rulespec-uk) instead of hand-coded variable logic. `Policy` wraps a compiled artifact; reforms and future-dated uprating assumptions are parameter overrides (`Policy::with_parameter`) that patch the versioned parameter table and recompile in memory in well under a millisecond. `Dataset` maps per-person input columns onto one tax year — including one-to-many relations such as income components per person (`with_relation` / `with_relation_input`) — and `calculate` runs the engine's dense columnar executor, returning output columns in person order. Ships a compiled income tax artifact covering the full ITA 2007 s.23 liability calculation for non-savings, non-dividend income: total income summed from income components via the s.23 Step 1 relation, s.24 reliefs deducted per component, the s.35 personal allowance (with the adjusted net income taper), and the s.10 rate bands at FA 2026 s.2 rates. A reform demo bench (`cargo bench --bench axiom_reform`) — a basic-rate +1p impact over 100k people: baseline eval, parameter patch, recompile, reform eval — runs end to end in under 50 ms. Also ships the Universal Credit artifact (WRA 2012 s.8 plus the UC Regulations 2013 component, housing, childcare, and income-deduction provisions, recomposed from rulespec-uk source) with a taper-rate reform demo (`cargo bench --bench axiom_uc`) over a 100k-family caseload in under 100 ms, exercising the Family root entity, boolean input columns, monthly assessment periods, and child/adult benefit-unit relations. National Insurance is covered by two further artifacts: primary Class 1 contributions (SSCBA 1992 s.8 bridged to the SSCR 2001 reg 10 weekly thresholds) evaluated over tax weeks via the new `Dataset::week` constructor, and Class 4 contributions on annual profits (SSCBA 1992 s.15, before the reg 100 annual maximum), each with a main-rate +1p reform demo (`cargo bench --bench axiom_nics`) over 100k people. Child benefit (SSCBA 1992 s.141 at the SI 2006/965 reg 2 weekly rates, enhanced rate for the eldest child) is evaluated per family over the s.141 responsibility relation, with an enhanced-rate reform demo (`cargo bench --bench axiom_child_benefit`). Pension credit's guarantee credit (SPCA 2002 s.2 with the SPC Regs 2002 reg 6 standard minimum guarantee and severe-disability and carer additional amounts) tops claimant income up to the appropriate minimum guarantee, with a single-rate reform demo (`cargo bench --bench axiom_pension_credit`); the s.3 savings credit is not yet composed. Savings and dividend rates (ITA 2007 ss.12-16) and s.26 reductions are the next slice. The backend now also sits invisibly behind the ordinary `Parameters`/`Simulation` interface: `Simulation::enable_axiom()` (surfaced as the `--axiom` CLI flag) translates the model's annual National Insurance parameters onto the statutory legal parameters (weekly thresholds as annual ÷ 52), runs Class 1 and Class 4 through the compiled artifacts, and writes the results back into the usual per-person microdata outputs — callers keep the existing annual parameters, reform overlays, and JSON output unchanged, and axiom results match the hand-coded engine exactly at baseline and under reform.
