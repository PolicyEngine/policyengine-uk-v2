Add the first slice of the axiom rules-engine backend (`src/axiom/`): policyengine-uk-rust can now evaluate statute-derived RuleSpec rules (composed from rulespec-uk) instead of hand-coded variable logic. `Policy` wraps a compiled artifact; reforms and future-dated uprating assumptions are parameter overrides (`Policy::with_parameter`) that patch the versioned parameter table and recompile in memory in well under a millisecond. `Dataset` maps per-person input columns onto one tax year — including one-to-many relations such as income components per person (`with_relation` / `with_relation_input`) — and `calculate` runs the engine's dense columnar executor, returning output columns in person order. Ships a compiled income tax artifact covering the full ITA 2007 s.23 liability calculation for non-savings, non-dividend income: total income summed from income components via the s.23 Step 1 relation, s.24 reliefs deducted per component, the s.35 personal allowance (with the adjusted net income taper), and the s.10 rate bands at FA 2026 s.2 rates. A reform demo bench (`cargo bench --bench axiom_reform`) — a basic-rate +1p impact over 100k people: baseline eval, parameter patch, recompile, reform eval — runs end to end in under 50 ms. Also ships the Universal Credit artifact (WRA 2012 s.8 plus the UC Regulations 2013 component, housing, childcare, and income-deduction provisions, recomposed from rulespec-uk source) with a taper-rate reform demo (`cargo bench --bench axiom_uc`) over a 100k-family caseload in under 100 ms, exercising the Family root entity, boolean input columns, monthly assessment periods, and child/adult benefit-unit relations. National Insurance is covered by two further artifacts: primary Class 1 contributions (SSCBA 1992 s.8 bridged to the SSCR 2001 reg 10 weekly thresholds) evaluated over tax weeks via the new `Dataset::week` constructor, and Class 4 contributions on annual profits (SSCBA 1992 s.15, before the reg 100 annual maximum), each with a main-rate +1p reform demo (`cargo bench --bench axiom_nics`) over 100k people. Child benefit (SSCBA 1992 s.141 at the SI 2006/965 reg 2 weekly rates, enhanced rate for the eldest child) is evaluated per family over the s.141 responsibility relation, with an enhanced-rate reform demo (`cargo bench --bench axiom_child_benefit`). Pension credit's guarantee credit (SPCA 2002 s.2 with the SPC Regs 2002 reg 6 standard minimum guarantee and severe-disability and carer additional amounts) tops claimant income up to the appropriate minimum guarantee, with a single-rate reform demo (`cargo bench --bench axiom_pension_credit`); the s.3 savings credit is not yet composed. Savings and dividend rates (ITA 2007 ss.12-16) and s.26 reductions are the next slice. The backend now also sits invisibly behind the ordinary `Parameters`/`Simulation` interface, on by default: every `Simulation` translates the model's parameters onto the statutory legal parameters at construction (annual NI thresholds as ÷ 52, weekly benefit rates as-is) and runs NI Class 1 and Class 4, child benefit, the pension credit guarantee credit, and universal credit through the compiled artifacts, writing the results back into the usual per-person and per-benunit microdata outputs before the benefit cap and household aggregation. The UC translation maps each benefit unit onto a Family row over the April assessment period (×12 back to annual): the model's monthly standard allowances, child elements, disabled child additions, work allowances, and taper rate patch the UC Regulations 2013 reg 22/36 parameters; reg 55 net earned income, reg 66 unearned income, the LHA rent cap, and the LCWRA/carer determinations are computed in the model and passed as inputs; and the two-child limit is applied through the per-child element flags. Claiming behaviour, eligibility gates, legacy-benefit migration, and the savings credit (s.3 not yet composed) stay in the model. Callers keep the existing annual parameters, reform overlays, and JSON output unchanged, and axiom results match the hand-coded formulas (retained as the verification reference for the equivalence tests) exactly at baseline and under reform. Artifact provenance is kept honest by `scripts/refresh_artifacts.sh`, which recomposes and recompiles all six artifacts from sibling rulespec-uk/axiom-compose/axiom-programs/axiom-rules-engine checkouts (engine at the Cargo.toml pin) and runs the test suite, and by a weekly `artifact-drift` CI workflow that regenerates against upstream HEAD and fails when the committed snapshots have gone stale, so upstream rule improvements surface as a red build prompting a refresh PR.
