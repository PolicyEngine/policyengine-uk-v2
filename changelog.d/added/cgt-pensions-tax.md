Port a coherent slice of HMRC capital gains tax and pensions tax (issue #45).

CGT (TCGA 1992): new band-aware `calculate_capital_gains_tax_banded` stacks gains (after the annual exempt amount) on top of `adjusted_net_income`, taxing the slice within the remaining basic-rate band at `basic_rate` and the rest at `higher_rate` (matching PolicyEngine-UK `capital_gains_tax`), reusing the existing residential-surcharge logic. Wired into the simulation Phase 2c, replacing the previous all-or-nothing higher/basic split. A configurable realisation behavioural response (`CgtResponseParams { elasticity, baseline_rate }`, new optional `cgt_response` parameter) scales realised gains by `(reform_rate / baseline_rate) ^ elasticity` before tax — the documented hook mirroring PolicyEngine-UK's log-difference `capital_gains_behavioural_response`. The static calc is complete; the response is a no-op until a reform sets `baseline_rate`, since there is no automatic baseline-vs-reform CGT plumbing threaded through `Simulation::new` yet (remaining work). The simpler `calculate_capital_gains_tax(person, params, is_higher_rate)` form is retained for the surcharge benchmark.

Pensions tax (FA 2004 Part 4): new optional `pensions` parameter (`PensionsParams`). Relief-at-source vs net-pay treatment of member contributions now flows into income tax — net pay reduces taxable earned income directly; relief at source extends the basic-rate band by the grossed-up contribution (`gross_up_contribution`, `extend_basic_rate_band`) to deliver higher-rate relief. Annual allowance with tapering (`tapered_annual_allowance`) and the annual-allowance charge (`annual_allowance_charge`) are added and applied at the member's marginal rate in income tax. Carry-forward of unused prior-year allowance is not modelled (simplification — the FRS does not record historical contributions).

2025/26 parameters set for `pensions` (annual allowance £60,000, taper from £260,000 adjusted income down to a £10,000 minimum) and `cgt_response` (static: elasticity 0). Unit tests cover worked examples for each: band-split CGT, residential surcharge in the higher band, the realisation response, net-pay vs relief-at-source income tax, the tapered allowance and the annual-allowance charge.
